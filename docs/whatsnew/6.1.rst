.. _whatsnew-6.1-table-column-order-from-rows:

Ordering of table columns constructed from ``rows``
===================================================

The order of columns as they appear in a ``Table`` constructed as ``Table(rows=...)``
may differ between ``astropy`` ``6.0`` and ``6.1``. Specifically, in ``6.0``,
then column ordering was determined by the first :class:`dict` if it contained values
for all the columns, or by sorting the column names alphabetically if it did not.
In ``6.1``, columns are simply inserted as they appear when the :class:`list` of
:class:`dict` is iterated over.

As an example,

    >>> data = [{'b': 10, 'c': 7, },
    ...         {'a': 15, 'c': 35, 'b': 20}]
    >>> t = Table(rows=data)
    >>> print(t)


would print, in ``6.0``

     a   b   c
    --- --- ---
     --  10   7
     15  20  35


and in ``6.1``

     b   c   a
    --- --- ---
     10   7  --
     20  35  15
